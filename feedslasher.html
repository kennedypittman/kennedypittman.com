FeedSlasher - AI-Curated News

Just gimme the good stuff

Act faster with news that matters

While other news apps drown you in headlines, FeedSlasher hand-picks the few articles you actually need. It understands exactly what you want to read and why, then delivers notes that you can quickly learn from or act on.

This isn't topic or keyword filtering. This is like having a personal assistant who curates your news for real strategic value—whether that's breaking updates, share-worthy content, competitor moves, emerging opportunities, or something else entirely unique to you.

Your digest arrives in Slack or email with concise summaries and personalized takeaways. You get the signal without the noise, act faster, and never miss a critical story.

I originally built this as my personal news digest. Now, I've now developed this into a fully functional web app. It is available to a small number of free users while I test and collect feedback. If this sounds interesting to you, let me know!

How It Works

1. Prep Your Assistant

Define your role, what you're interested in, and how you will use the information. Pick a selection of news feeds for the AI to monitor daily.

2. AI Picks Your Best News

Knowing your custom profile, FeedSlasher uses AI to analyze each day's articles. News posts are scored by a proprietary formula, weighing how each aligns to your needs.

3. AI Creates Summaries

The top scored articles become your daily picks. For these, the AI reads the entire articles and generates your personalized summary and key takeaway.

4. Get Actionable News

Receive a daily slack message or email with your curated news. Just the best articles for your needs - quickly digestable.

Example Use Cases

FeedSlasher's AI considers who, what, and why as it analyzes and scores daily articles.

A SaaS marketer wants to write shareworthy posts on LinkedIn. FeedSlasher selects timely industry news that is topical, inspires original commentary, prioritizes viral potential, and that would be interesting to CTOs.

A startup founder in AI healthcare wants to track early-stage funding and FDA policy shifts. FeedSlasher surfaces competitor launches, investment moves, regulatory developments —so the founder spots opportunities early.

A policy researcher needs to track legislation, regulatory updates, and advocacy movements across jurisdictions. FeedSlasher pulls from legal, governmental, and nonprofit feeds—surfacing only the updates that match their focus—so they can stay current, brief stakeholders, and respond quickly to emerging policy shifts.
