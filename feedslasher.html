Cut Through The Noise

Even with the best filtering, reading business news is a chore and a slog.

Yet, my work depends on timely and actionable news updates. So...

I built my own AI-powered news curator and summarization tool.

Look, I'm not a total idiot. As a business idea, this has a lot going against it. And yet - it's been very useful. Now I'm exploring how this might have value for others.

Forget keyword filtering. This is an AI assistant that knows YOU, WHAT you want to read, and WHY.

Get the top news that aligns with your goals, along with AI summaries, so you can stay productive and act fast.

How It Works

Train Your AI Assistant

Define your role, what you're interested in, and how you will use the information. Pick a selection of news feeds for the AI to watch.

AI Curates The News

Knowing your needs, your AI assistant analyzes each day's articles. They are scored by how they align to your needs.

AI Creates Summaries

For the daily picks, the AI generates your personalized summary and key takeaway.

Get Actionable News

Receive a daily slack message or email with your curated news links, AI summaries, and key takeaway. Just the best articles - quickly digestable.

Example Use Cases

A marketer wants to write shareworthy thought-leadership posts on LinkedIn. FeedSlasher selects timely news articles that are topical, inspire original commentary, prioritize viral potential, and that would be interesting to the marketer's target audiences.
A startup founder in AI healthcare wants to track early-stage funding and FDA policy shifts. FeedSlasher surfaces only the most relevant updates—competitor launches, investment moves, regulatory developments—so the founder stays ahead, spots opportunities early, and makes faster strategic decisions.
A policy researcher focused on digital privacy needs to track legislation, regulatory updates, and advocacy movements across jurisdictions. FeedSlasher pulls from a handpicked set of legal, governmental, and nonprofit feeds—surfacing only the updates that match their focus—so they can stay current, brief stakeholders, and respond quickly to emerging policy shifts.
