Cut Through The Noise

An AI-powered news assistant that knows your EXACT needs, which will surface and summarize your most important news.

If you want a news feed with custom filters, this is NOT for you. 10,000 other apps do that.

This is for people who need "hand picked" news for a particular use case, and quickly digestible.

Unlike keyword filters, FeedSlasher's AI can also prioritize atributes like viral sharing potential, instructional value, breaking news, strategic importance, competitive insights, etc.

You'll get the most useful daily news, sent to Slack or email, with an AI summary and a personalized key takeaway - so you can act fast.

Currently, this app only exists as my personal productivity tool. It's valuable to me, but this is an insanely crowded market space - so I need to know if this sounds interesting to you!

How It Works

Train Your AI Assistant

Define your role, what you're interested in, and how you will use the information. Pick a selection of news feeds for the AI to watch.

AI Curates The News

Knowing your needs, your AI assistant analyzes each day's articles. They are scored by how they align to your needs.

AI Creates Summaries

For the daily picks, the AI generates your personalized summary and key takeaway.

Get Actionable News

Receive a daily slack message or email with your curated news links, AI summaries, and key takeaway. Just the best articles - quickly digestable.

Example Use Cases

A marketer wants to write shareworthy posts on LinkedIn. FeedSlasher selects timely news articles that are topical, inspire original commentary, prioritize viral potential, and that would be interesting to the marketer's target audiences.
A startup founder in AI healthcare wants to track early-stage funding and FDA policy shifts. FeedSlasher surfaces competitor launches, investment moves, regulatory developments—so the founder spots opportunities early.
A policy researcher needs to track legislation, regulatory updates, and advocacy movements across jurisdictions. FeedSlasher pulls from legal, governmental, and nonprofit feeds—surfacing only the updates that match their focus—so they can stay current, brief stakeholders, and respond quickly to emerging policy shifts.
